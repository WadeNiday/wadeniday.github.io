Crude oil

Crude oil stocks increased by 0.4 MMBbls in the week ended 7/3/15 with modest declines in the Gulf Coast, Midwest and Rocky Mountain regions offset by increases in East Coast and West stocks. Days supply increased to 28.3 days from 28.1 days in the week previous which compares to 24.1 days in the year ago period. Production increased to 9.604 MMBpd (9.598 MMBpd on moving 4-week average). Crude oil imports declined to 7.316 MMBpd from 7.513 MMBpd in the previous week. Additions to distillate and gasoline stocks were significant given despite a decline in refinery utilization to 94.7% from 95.0% in the week previous.

Natural gas

Working gas in storage increased 91 Bcf to 2,668Bcf. Working gas in storage now exceeds the 5-year average level by approximately 2%. Prices continue to demonstrate weakness with the front month contract declining from $2.78/MMBtu to $2.69/MMBtu. Spot prices at regional hubs declined markedly as well.

Capital markets access

There were no new transactions this past week although a $175 million deal for Petroceltic International was being marketed. A number of credits continue to be mentioned as potential “bailout” candidates.
