think big

A solid design process provides a framework that is consistent enough to advance large-scale projects, yet flexible enough
to improve upon the smallest detail. Maintaining a process helps keep the project focused. It increases workflow efficiency and offers a feedback loop for collaborative improvements.

  • Four things to always remember
  • Content is king
  • Simplicity is the goal
  • Critique is not attack
  • People matter most
  • Understand the problem

  • A clearly defined problem and
    a firm understanding into any research is critical in setting the
    tone for the project.

  • Sketch lo-fi ideas

  • Itʼs not pretty, but getting ideas
    on to paper is a way to accelerate
    thinking. For me itʼs both writing
    and sketching.

  • Prototype hi-fi concepts

  • This is where ideas sink or swim.
    Visual details begin to bring
    the system to life, yet the look
    and feel keeps evolving.

  • Design and refine

  • The prototype and feedback
    loop are now leading the charge towards simplicity, refinement,
    and clarity. Keep refining.

HFMA

This app serves as a resource for healthcare finance professionals by providing standard equations for measure revenue cycle. After the launch screen, a user would chose the desired practice area. The formulas are then listed by category and can be favorited for quick reference. Further information is provided to educate users about the 'Map' product suite or to contact the team. Available in the App Store and Google Play.

My role began with selling the idea into the current product suite and included user experience, visual design, and user interface; as well as selecting a development parter to collaborate on the prototyping, testing, build-out, and shipment of the final product.

DivineBackNine.com

This mobile app concept would allow golfers to search and book tee times within the divinebacknine.com golf network.the intent is to show the flow of booking golf for the first time user without getting too carried away with setting up the account.

The concept began out of a personal desire to streamline the existing desktop experience. My role has included designing the user interface — including thinking through the interactions — as well as the writing, and putting (photo). I've done user testing with other golfers and plan to build a prototype while searching for a willing dev to help bring this to life. Let me know if you know of any willing folks!
